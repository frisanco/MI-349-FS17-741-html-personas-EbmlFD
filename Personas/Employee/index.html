Name

Megan Process

Descriptor

Current Earth Beyond Employee

Quote

"My team is currently working with contractors on these rocket booster blueprints."

"Would it be possible to take a two week vacation? I am, after all, a 5 year veteran."

Who is it?

Age 32; Helpful, kind, but hard-working in every sense of the term; wants success for herself, as well as Earth Beyond.

Title: Team Lead; works with assigned project team within Earth Beyond.

What Goals?

Personal achievement. Megan has worked hard to obtain the position she currently holds; productivity and personal goal setting are huge parts of her work ethic.

Megan works positively and is always willing to learn. Sites and shops should be easy to understand, but she's willing to learn and improve processes when necessary.

What Attitude?

Similar to Gary, she expects professionalism, courtesy, but also some leeway in regard to strictness.

Being close to her team, she often finds herself cross-referencing her colleagues findings on the web.

Which Behavior?

Clear organization and communication are a MUST. Information must be able to be accessed quickly in order to keep a full flow of communication between her and her colleagues.

Frustrations arise when processes show promise, but are not improved. Megan wants to see the best for her team, but her time is limited.

Trends

Retro and mainstream websites catch Megan's eye. Unlike those in higher management positions who seek perfection, she is willing to accept less.

Functional Importance

Functionality is a HUGE plus to Megan, as well as her team, as every person should not have to worry about frustrations when accessing shared information.

Decision Making

Megan often takes time to evaluate a decision. Her time is important, but the success of her team is even more important.

Facts or Emotion?

More decisions are based on emotion. The "language" of the team often influences Megan's decisions in future conflicts.
